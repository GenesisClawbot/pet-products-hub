Best Dog Grooming Tools of 2026

Skip the groomer bill. These tools do the job at home — from deshedding to nail trimming and everything in between.

Why Groom Your Dog at Home

Professional grooming adds up fast. A single full groom can run forty to eighty pounds depending on your location and the size of your dog. A German Shepherd that blows coat twice a year can bury a professional in fur. A dog with anxiety who hates the groomer is stressful for everyone involved.

Building a basic home grooming kit costs about the same as two professional appointments. Once you have the tools, maintenance grooming takes thirty minutes every few weeks rather than a three-hour ordeal. The trick is getting the right tools for your dog is coat type. A slicker brush meant for a poodle will not work on a short-haired boxer, and vice versa.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Home grooming saves significant money over time
  • Regular brushing reduces loose hair around the home
  • Checking ears and nails weekly catches problems early
  • Enzymatic ear cleaners actually work on smelly ears

Cons

  • Nail trimming requires confidence — one bad cut can cause bleeding
  • Deshedding tools can damage coat if used too aggressively
  • Cheap nail clippers dull quickly and crush nails
  • Some dogs simply will not tolerate home grooming without training

Verdict

The Furminator earns its reputation for heavy shedders — Labrador owners, this is for you. The Chris Christensen slicker brush is the one to get if you have a long-haired breed prone to mats. For nail care, invest in quality clippers and go slow. If your dog hates grooming, start young and keep sessions short. Ten minutes of brushing with treats beats a traumatic monthly groom.
