Why Your Dog's Bed Matters More Than You Think

Dogs sleep 12 to 14 hours a day. That is a lot of time spent on whatever surface you give them. A cheap bed flattens within weeks, leaving joints on hard floor. An older dog with arthritis will struggle without proper support. Even young, healthy dogs benefit from a bed that keeps them warm in winter and cool in summer.

How to Pick the Right Bed

Measure your dog from nose to tail base and add 10 to 20 centimetres. They need room to stretch out or curl up depending on their style. If your dog is over 25 kilograms or over six years old, prioritise orthopedic memory foam. For dogs who love to lean, a bed with bolsters works well. If you have two dogs, size up — sharing a bed sounds cute until neither gets proper sleep.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Memory foam beds relieve joint pressure for senior dogs
  • Cooling mats reduce overheating in summer
  • Elevated beds last longer and are easy to clean
  • Waterproof liners protect against accidents and drool

Cons

  • Quality orthopedic beds cost more upfront
  • Foam beds trap hair and require regular washing
  • Cooling mats need a cool floor to work properly
  • Some dogs simply prefer the sofa regardless
